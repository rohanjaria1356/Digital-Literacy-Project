# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist

> A practical checklist of **10 actionable tips** for college students in India to protect themselves from cybercrime.

---

## General Online Safety

### 1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords for Every Account
- Combine uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters (e.g., `C$e_2026!Bhopal`)
- Never reuse the same password across platforms
- Use a password manager like Bitwarden (free) or Google Password Manager to keep track

### 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Everywhere
- Turn on 2FA for Gmail, Instagram, GitHub, LinkedIn, and banking apps
- Prefer authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator) over SMS-based OTPs when possible
- This ensures that even if your password is leaked, your account remains protected

### 3. Never Click Suspicious Links in Emails, SMS, or WhatsApp
- Check the sender's email address carefully — scammers use addresses like `support@g00gle.com` (with zeros) or `sbi-alerts@random.in`
- Hover over links before clicking to see the actual URL destination
- When in doubt, go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself

### 4. Keep Your Devices and Apps Updated
- Install OS updates and app updates promptly — they often patch critical security vulnerabilities
- Enable automatic updates on your phone and laptop
- Uninstall apps you no longer use — every installed app is a potential attack surface

### 5. Be Cautious on Public Wi-Fi (Hostel, Café, Railway Station)
- Avoid logging into banking apps, UPI, or email on public/shared Wi-Fi networks
- If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) for encrypted browsing
- Always log out of accounts when using shared or public computers

---

## 💳 Financial / UPI Safety

### 6. Never Share Your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with Anyone
- **No bank, payment app, or government agency will ever ask for your PIN or OTP** — this is a universal rule
- If someone calls claiming to be from your bank and asks for these details, hang up immediately
- Report the number to your bank and on the Cyber Crime Portal

### 7. Verify Before Accepting Any UPI Collect/Payment Request
- A "Collect Request" is someone **asking you to pay** — not sending you money
- Always read the transaction details on screen before entering your PIN
- If a stranger on OLX or a marketplace asks you to "accept" a payment, it is almost certainly a scam
- **You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money**

## Social Media & Identity Protection

### 8. Review Your Social Media Privacy Settings Monthly
- Set profiles to private or restrict who can view your posts and personal information
- Remove old posts that reveal too much personal data (location, routine, financial details)
- Be wary of quizzes and games that ask for personal trivia — these are often designed to harvest security question answers

### 9. Don't Share Sensitive Personal Information Online
- Never post photos of your Aadhaar card, PAN card, college ID, boarding passes, or financial documents
- Avoid sharing your exact location in real-time (hostel check-ins, live travel updates)
- Be cautious about sharing your class schedule — this reveals when you're away from your room

---

## Emergency Response

### 10. Know How to Report Cybercrime Immediately
If you become a victim of any cybercrime, act fast:

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Report Online** | [National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint with evidence |
| **Call Helpline** | **1930** — National Cyber Crime Helpline (available **24×7**) |
| **Contact Your Bank** | Call your bank's customer care immediately to freeze your account if money was stolen |
| **File an FIR** | Visit your nearest police station with screenshots and transaction IDs as evidence |

> ** The first 24 hours are critical.** The faster you report financial fraud, the higher the chances of recovering your money.
