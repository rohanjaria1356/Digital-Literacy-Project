# Task 5 — Cybercrime Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where criminals trick victims into authorising unauthorised payments through apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, or BHIM. Unlike traditional banking fraud that requires account numbers and passwords, UPI fraud exploits the simplicity and speed of UPI transactions — often needing just a single tap from the victim to transfer money.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **The Setup:** The fraudster identifies a target, often through online marketplace listings (OLX, Facebook Marketplace) or by impersonating a bank official or customer service representative via phone call or WhatsApp message.

2. **Building Trust:** The scammer poses as a buyer interested in purchasing an item the victim has listed for sale, or as a bank employee warning about "suspicious activity" on the victim's account.

3. **The Trick:** Instead of sending money, the fraudster sends a **UPI collect request** (a payment demand) to the victim, claiming that accepting the request will credit money to their account. Alternatively, they may ask the victim to scan a QR code — which is actually a payment request, not a receive request.

4. **The Execution:** The victim, believing they are receiving money, enters their UPI PIN to "accept" the request. The moment the PIN is entered, money is debited from their account and transferred to the fraudster.

5. **The Disappearance:** Once the transaction is completed, the fraudster blocks the victim's number and vanishes. The money is quickly moved through multiple accounts or withdrawn, making recovery extremely difficult.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- College students selling items online (textbooks, electronics)
- First-time UPI users unfamiliar with the difference between "send" and "collect" requests
- Senior citizens who are less tech-savvy
- Small business owners and street vendors who recently adopted digital payments

## Consequences

- **Financial loss** ranging from hundreds to lakhs of rupees
- **Emotional distress** and loss of trust in digital payments
- **Difficulty in recovery** — banks often cannot reverse UPI transactions since the user "authorised" them by entering their PIN
- **Legal complications** — filing an FIR and pursuing the case can take months with no guarantee of resolution

## Source

This case study is based on patterns reported by the **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** in their awareness campaigns and incidents documented on the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** (cybercrime.gov.in). The RBI has repeatedly warned that "No legitimate entity will ever ask for your UPI PIN or OTP."
